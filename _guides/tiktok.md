TikTok is one of the most data-intensive apps in widespread use. It collects extensive information including device identifiers, location data, browsing habits, keystroke patterns, and content you view — even content you don't engage with. If you work in a higher-risk field or value privacy, carefully consider whether you need a TikTok account at all.

As of early 2026, TikTok's US operations are controlled by a new American-majority entity (TikTok USDS), with US user data stored on Oracle's cloud infrastructure. While this addresses some data sovereignty concerns, the app's data collection practices remain extensive regardless of where the data is stored.

## Use a Strong Password

TikTok's minimum password requirements are weak. Use a long, complex, unique password generated by a password manager. Do not reuse passwords from other accounts — TikTok accounts are frequently targeted by credential stuffing attacks.

## Set Up Passkeys

TikTok now supports passkey sign-in. Passkeys let you authenticate using your device's biometrics (Face ID, fingerprint) or PIN instead of a password, and are resistant to phishing attacks.

To set up a passkey, go to **Profile → Menu (☰) → Settings and privacy → Account → Security → Passkeys** and follow the prompts. Only set up passkeys on devices you own and control.

## Enable Two-Step Verification

TikTok offers 2-step verification (2SV) via SMS, email, or authenticator app. **Use an authenticator app** (such as Google Authenticator, Authy, or Microsoft Authenticator) rather than SMS to protect against SIM swap attacks.

To enable: go to **Profile → Menu (☰) → Settings and privacy → Account → Security → 2-step verification**. Select your preferred methods and follow the setup prompts. You can enable multiple methods as fallbacks, but make sure at least one is an authenticator app.

Save any backup/recovery codes in your password manager.

## Make Your Account Private

If privacy is part of your security model, switch your account to private. This means only approved followers can see your content.

Go to **Profile → Menu (☰) → Settings and privacy → Privacy → Private account** and toggle it on.

Even with a private account, be aware that your profile photo, username, and bio remain publicly visible. Keep these free of identifying information if you want to minimize your exposure.

## Privacy Settings

Navigate to **Profile → Menu (☰) → Settings and privacy → Privacy** and review every option. Key settings to lock down:

-   **Discoverability** - Review whether your account can be suggested to others, found via contacts, or recommended to Facebook friends. Toggle all of these off.
-   **Contacts syncing** - Do not sync your contacts. If you already have, disconnect them. This protects the privacy of everyone in your address book.
-   **Who can send you direct messages** - Set to "No one" or "Friends" (mutual followers). TikTok DMs are a common vector for phishing and scams.
-   **Who can Stitch/Duet with your videos** - Set to "Friends" or "Only me" to prevent your content from being reused in ways you don't control.
-   **Who can view your liked videos** - Set to "Only me".
-   **Who can view your following list** - Set to "Only me" or "Friends".
-   **Who can comment on your videos** - Set to "Friends" or "No one" if you don't need public engagement.
-   **Who can download your videos** - Set to off. This prevents your content from being saved and redistributed.
-   **Who can view your profile** - Review and restrict as needed.

## Ad Personalization and Data Tracking

TikTok heavily personalizes ads based on your activity both on and off the app. Go to **Profile → Menu (☰) → Settings and privacy → Ads** and disable:

-   **Ad personalization** - Toggle this off to limit how TikTok targets ads based on your activity.
-   **Use of off-TikTok activity for ad targeting** - Toggle this off to prevent advertisers from using data about your activity on other websites and apps.

Additionally, use the **Disconnect Advertisers** feature to stop specific advertisers from using their off-TikTok data about you. You can also use the **Clear My Activity** feature to disconnect any off-TikTok activity data already shared by advertising partners.

## Manage Connected Apps and Login Methods

Review which third-party apps and services are connected to your TikTok account. Go to **Profile → Menu (☰) → Settings and privacy → Account → Security → Manage app permissions** and revoke access for any apps you don't actively use.

Also review your **Login with TikTok** connections — if you've used TikTok to sign into other services, each connection is an additional point of exposure.

## Monitor Login Activity

Regularly review your active sessions. Go to **Profile → Menu (☰) → Settings and privacy → Account → Security → Your devices** (or **Manage devices**). Remove any device or session you don't recognize or no longer use.

TikTok also sends **Security alerts** for suspicious login attempts or account changes. Review these notifications promptly and take action if you see anything unexpected.

## Download Your Data

You can request a copy of all data TikTok holds about you. Go to **Profile → Menu (☰) → Settings and privacy → Account → Download your data**. Choose a format (TXT or JSON) and tap **Request data**. The file is usually ready within a few days and includes your profile info, activity history, and direct messages. Note that your actual video files are not included — save those manually if needed.

Reviewing your data export is a good way to understand the extent of what TikTok collects about you.

## How to Delete Your Account

If you decide to delete your TikTok account:

1.  Go to **Profile → Menu (☰) → Settings and privacy → Account → Deactivate or delete account**.
2.  Select **Delete account** and follow the prompts.
3.  Enter your password or verify via your linked phone/email.

Before deleting, download your data and save any videos you want to keep. After deletion, there is a **30-day grace period** during which you can restore your account by logging back in. After 30 days, TikTok permanently deletes your account and associated data.

## General Recommendations

-   **Be cautious with links in DMs and comments.** TikTok is heavily used for phishing, especially targeting crypto users and influencers. Never click links from unknown accounts.
-   **Don't reveal personal info in videos.** Background details (home address, workplace, license plates, school names) can be extracted from video content. Be mindful of what's visible.
-   **Disable location tagging** on your posts if it's enabled.
-   **Review content preferences.** Go to **Settings and privacy → Content preferences** and disable any features that share your viewing habits or activity status.
-   **Consider using TikTok in a browser** rather than the native app if you want to limit the data it collects. The app has significantly more access to device sensors, contacts, clipboard, and location than the web version.
