Facebook is owned by Meta and is deeply integrated with Instagram, Messenger, and WhatsApp through the Meta Accounts Center. It collects extensive data about your activity both on and off the platform. If you work in a higher-risk field like cryptocurrency, be especially cautious about what your profile reveals — Facebook profiles are a prime source for social engineering reconnaissance.

## Consider Deleting Your Facebook

If you aren't actively using Facebook, the single most effective way to protect yourself is to [permanently delete your account](https://www.facebook.com/deactivate_delete_account/). Deactivation hides your profile but Meta retains your data. Deletion permanently removes it (after a 30-day grace period, with up to 90 days for full removal from backups).

Before deleting, download your data via **Settings and privacy → Accounts Center → Your information and permissions → Download your information**. This is also currently the most reliable way to ensure your data is not used for Meta's AI training if your objection request is denied.

If you choose to keep your account, read on.

## Use a Strong Password

Use a long, complex, unique password generated by a password manager. Do not reuse passwords from other accounts. Facebook accounts are high-value targets for credential stuffing attacks. To change your password, go to **Settings and privacy → Accounts Center → Password and security → Change password**.

## Set Up Passkeys

Facebook now supports passkey sign-in. Passkeys let you authenticate using your device's biometrics (Face ID, fingerprint) or PIN instead of a password, and are resistant to phishing attacks.

To set up a passkey, go to **Settings and privacy → Accounts Center → Password and security → Passkeys** and follow the prompts. Only set up passkeys on devices you own and control.

## Enable Two-Factor Authentication

Facebook offers several 2FA methods: authenticator app, SMS, WhatsApp, and security keys. **Use an authenticator app** (such as Google Authenticator, Authy, or Microsoft Authenticator) or a **hardware security key**. Avoid SMS and WhatsApp due to SIM swap risks.

To enable: go to **Settings and privacy → Accounts Center → Password and security → Two-factor authentication**. Select your Facebook account and choose your preferred method.

Save your recovery codes in your password manager in case you lose access to your authenticator app.

## Remove Your Phone Number

Go to **Settings and privacy → Accounts Center → Personal details** and remove your mobile phone number if one is listed. With a SIM swap attack, an attacker could use your phone number to reset your password or bypass 2FA. If you need a phone number for 2FA, use an authenticator app instead and then remove the number.

## Enable Login Alerts

Turn on alerts for unrecognized logins so you're notified whenever someone signs into your account from a new device or browser. Go to **Settings and privacy → Accounts Center → Password and security → Login alerts** and enable notifications via email or push notification.

Also review **Where you're logged in** from the same menu. Remove any session you don't recognize or no longer use.

## See What the Public Can See About You

Anyone with a Facebook account can potentially view your photos, friends list, tagged locations, and things you've liked. Check what your profile looks like to the public by using Facebook's **"View As"** feature: go to your profile, click the three dots (⋯) below your cover photo, and select **View As**.

Review what's visible and lock down anything you don't want public.

## Privacy Settings

Facebook's defaults are not privacy-friendly. Navigate to **Settings and privacy → Privacy** (or visit [facebook.com/settings?tab=privacy](https://www.facebook.com/settings?tab=privacy)) and configure:

-   **Who can see your future posts** - Set to "Friends" or more restrictive.
-   **Who can see your friends list** - Set to "Only me". Your friends list is useful to social engineers.
-   **Who can look you up using your email address** - Set to "Only me" or "Friends".
-   **Who can look you up using your phone number** - Set to "Only me" or "Friends".
-   **Do you want search engines outside of Facebook to link to your profile** - Set to "No".
-   **Limit past posts** - Use the "Limit Past Posts" button to retroactively change the audience for all your old public posts to "Friends" in one step.

## Privacy Checkup

Facebook provides a [Privacy Checkup](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/checkup/) that walks you through your sharing settings, account security, data preferences, and ad settings in a guided flow. Run through this at least quarterly — Facebook frequently changes defaults and adds new sharing features that may reset your preferences.

## Review and Remove Apps

Do not use Facebook to log into other sites if you can avoid it — each "Login with Facebook" connection gives that app access to your profile data and expands your attack surface.

Review connected apps at **Settings and privacy → Settings → Apps and websites** (or visit [facebook.com/settings?tab=applications](https://www.facebook.com/settings?tab=applications)). Remove any app you no longer use or don't recognize. Pay attention to the permissions each app holds.

## Ad Preferences and Tracking

Navigate to **Settings and privacy → Accounts Center → Ad preferences** and restrict as much as possible:

-   **Ad topics** - Review and hide ad topics you don't want to be targeted with.
-   **Advertiser settings** - Review advertisers who have uploaded contact lists that include you, and hide ads from specific advertisers.
-   **Ad settings** - Disable data-based ad targeting options including "Data about your activity from partners", "Categories used to reach you", and "Audience-based advertising".

### Off-Facebook Activity

This is one of the most important privacy settings. Facebook tracks your activity on other websites and apps through its tracking pixel and SDK. Go to **Settings and privacy → Settings → Your Facebook information → Off-Facebook activity** (or visit [facebook.com/off_facebook_activity](https://www.facebook.com/off_facebook_activity)). From here you can:

-   **Clear history** - Disconnect your off-Facebook activity from your account.
-   **Manage future activity** - Toggle off "Future off-Facebook activity" to prevent new tracking data from being linked to your account going forward.

## Protecting Your Data From Meta AI

Meta uses your public Facebook posts, photos, and comments to train its generative AI models. **This is enabled by default**, and there is no simple on/off toggle.

### Submit a data objection

Navigate to **Settings and privacy → Privacy Center → How Meta uses information for generative AI models and features**, scroll down, and click **"Right to object"**. Fill out the form with your email address and submit. You can also access this directly at [facebook.com/privacy/genai](https://www.facebook.com/privacy/genai).

For users in the EU, EEA, and UK, there is a formal GDPR-based objection process. For US users, the process exists but Meta is not legally required to honor it.

Be aware that opting out only covers future data — any data already used for training before your objection cannot be removed from the model.

### Minimize your exposure

The most effective steps you can take:

-   **Make all your posts "Friends only"** — Meta says it does not use content from private posts for AI training.
-   **Use "Limit Past Posts"** to retroactively change old public posts to Friends-only.
-   **Minimize public profile information** — review your bio, workplace, education, and other fields visible to the public.
-   If Meta denies your objection request, **deleting your account** remains the only guaranteed way to prevent your data from being used.

## Meta Accounts Center

Be aware that many security and privacy settings have moved to the **Meta Accounts Center**, which manages Facebook, Instagram, and Messenger together. You can access it at [accountscenter.facebook.com](https://accountscenter.facebook.com) or via **Settings and privacy → Accounts Center**. Key items managed here include password and security, personal details, linked accounts, and data download/deletion.

If you've linked your Instagram account, changes you make in the Accounts Center may affect both platforms. Review linked accounts and consider whether you want them connected.

## General Recommendations

-   **Be skeptical of friend requests from people you don't know.** Fake profiles are commonly used to gather information about you and your contacts.
-   **Review your tagged photos and posts.** Go to **Settings and privacy → Profile and tagging** and enable review for tags before they appear on your profile.
-   **Limit who can post on your timeline** to "Friends" or "Only me".
-   **Turn off facial recognition** if the option is available in your region (Settings → Face recognition).
-   **Revisit these settings quarterly.** Facebook frequently introduces new features that default to the least private option.
